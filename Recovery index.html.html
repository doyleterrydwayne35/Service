Crime Trend Update

URGENT: HUMAN TRAFFICKING-FUELED SCAM CENTRES

Global law enforcement agencies report alarming rise in organized crime networks using trafficking victims to operate large-scale scam operations.

Global Threat Overview

Transnational criminal organizations are establishing fraudulent call centers in multiple countries, staffed by victims of human trafficking forced to commit financial scams:

  • Primarily located in Southeast Asia but operating globally
  • Victims are lured with fake job offers then held captive
  • Operations target victims in Europe, North America, and Australia
  • Estimated $billions in losses to victims worldwide

Modus Operandi

Criminal networks employ sophisticated methods to exploit victims and target new victims:

  • Romance scams and fake investment schemes
  • Impersonation of government officials (tax, immigration)
  • Tech support fraud and cryptocurrency investment scams
  • Victims forced to work 12-18 hour shifts under threat of violence
  • Compounds guarded by armed personnel with restricted movement

International Response

Interpol-coordinated operations targeting these criminal networks:

  • Operation Storm Makers II (2023): 281 arrests across 27 countries
  • Rescue of over 1,500 trafficking victims in coordinated raids
  • Disruption of 135 scam centers across multiple jurisdictions
  • Enhanced intelligence sharing through Interpol channels
  • Financial tracking of illicit proceeds through cryptocurrency

Global Impact Map

Regions most affected by human trafficking-fueled scam operations:

Asia-Pacific

  • Myanmar: 120+ known scam compounds
  • Cambodia: Major operations in Sihanoukville
  • Laos: Emerging hotspot along Mekong River
  • Philippines: Operations in Manila and Clark
  • Thailand: Border regions with Myanmar

Other Regions

  • Eastern Europe: Operations in Ukraine and Moldova
  • Middle East: UAE as financial hub
  • Africa: Operations in Nigeria and South Africa
  • Latin America: Mexico and Colombia emerging
